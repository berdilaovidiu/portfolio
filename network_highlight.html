LAYERS OF INFORMATION IN NETWORKS

PROBLEM TO SOLVE

Networks used in biology are most of the times big, with a lot of overlapping nodes, edges, text and a lot of colors. They tend to look like hairballs and making sense of a hairball is most of the times a challenging task.

One way to improve the process is to emphasize the relevant information and hide the rest. This is nothing new, most of the interactive networks implementations do it.

There was something specific to this case though, I was not allowed to override the colors of the network's elements. I was only allowed to use transparency and additional colors, as long as the original information was still visible.

Another requirement was to come up with a solution that would scale up to multiple adjacent levels and convey the information hierarchy. This translates to emphasizing gradually, in a decreasing order, the nodes of interest, their closest neighbours, the more distant neighbours, and blend the rest of the network in an almost invisible layer, in the background.

PROPOSED SOLUTIONS

The no brainer was to fade out anything that is not in the region of interest, emphasizing interesting information this way. The solution preservers the colors and is also scalable by using different levels of transparencies for the adjacent elements.

The second solution is just an enhanced version of the previous one. Instead of changing the transparency abruptly from level to level, I changed it gradually. This still preserves the hierarchy and also creates a 3D effect, lifting up the nodes of interest from the rest of the network

The third solution is quite different and it involves surrounding the elements of interest with a yellow glow. Adding more color to an already dense visualization was not helping my case. I fixed it by taking out some of the "chart junk", as Edward Tufte would call it.

Edges are important, but they use a lot of ink. I reduced the ink usage by taking away some of the color from the middle of the edges and kept the meaning by leveraging the closure and good continuation principles from the Gestalt psychology.

RESULT

I tested all the proposed solutions with real data and in the end we decided to implement the simplest one. There are two levels of transparency, the elements of interest are opaque and the rest of the network fades away in the background.

CONCLUSIONS

This was the first step of series of improvements made to the networks in the application. Following changes were related to improving the legibility of the text and offering multiple layout choices, some of them sensible to node selections.

The changes were rolled out during multiple releases and this was a perfect way to deliver since networks are the core part of the application and are used by the vast majority of the users.
