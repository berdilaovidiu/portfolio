ISOPROFILER

THE NEED

When I got involved in this project, a beta version had already been released and the feedback received from the early adopters was not great, both in terms of functionality and usability. The plan was to analyze the findings and come up with an improved design to take care of the shortcomings of the first prototype.

The primary need was way for scientists analyze RNA-Seq experiments in order to find interesting isoforms, along with their involvement in various diseases and biological functions, all backed by published evidence.

PREVIOUS BETA VERSION

The prototype released as a beta version had already done the heavy lifting in terms of converting the datasets to the right format, creating the data structures, analyzing the data and finding the evindence from a large knowledge base of manually curated content.

The major issue was the presentation of the results that was done by the way of a huge filterable table with a two level hierarchy. More than that, a large part of the input data couldn't be analyzed because by that point not a lot had been published on isoforms, so it was left out from the results, creating confusion and indignation for the users.

RESEARCH

Since I was not familiar with the biological context around the new feature, I had to do a lot of research to truly understand what isoforms are, how they are measured, the limitations of the current technologies and the challenges scientists face when dealing with the new type of experiment data. This gave me a pretty good idea on the tools and processes, as well as the terminology and the visual language used in the filed.

Having done the research, I was ready to review the user interviews done on the beta release. Luckily, I had recordings of three participants who offered a lot of good feedback that allowed me to identify the main pain points and some interesting new opportunities.

The main takaways were:

  • focus needs to be on the isoform expression data instead of the little literature evidence available
  • the large volumes of data are hard to digest and a drill down approach is preferable
  • there are very specific data patterns of interest

VISUAL ISOFORM REPRESENTATION

In order to make the interesting expression data patterns easy to spot, I took a more visual approach and experimented with multiple ways of displaying quantitative data

The solution needed to work for singular experiments, as well as for multiple experiments that were being analyzed side by side.
