RXP Overhaul

The Rezolve Experience Platform (RXP) is a web application that allows merchants to create promotional content. This content can take the form of mobile landing pages, lead generation forms, and purchasable products. Merchants can then promote their content via mobile, web, or physical experiences.

I joined the company right when RXP was undergoing a redesign. The old design lacked extensibility and had poor responsiveness. Furthermore, it looked outdated.

In less than three months, I coordinated and contributed to a complete redesign. We started with a thorough usability audit and addressed the most important issues based on severity and frequency. The end result was a fresh and user-friendly design that was highly mobile-friendly and modular, all based on a well documented design system.

Unfortunately, the redesign did not come to fruition due to a sudden change in the company's focus and priorities. Despite this setback, we didn't let our efforts go to waste. Instead, we used our findings from the usability audit to identify lower-effort solutions that could be iteratively implemented in the live version of the product. By doing so, we were able to make incremental improvements to the platform without the need for a full redesign.

RXP Refresh

Due to our limited budget and resources, we focused on implementing small, incremental changes that would improve the overall user experience. While enhancing the aesthetics of our platform was one of our goals, we recognized that consistency, visual hierarchy, and informative empty states were critical factors in addressing usability issues.

Taking a baby steps approach was essential to the success of our project. We began by updating basic design elements such as typography, buttons, and navigation bars. Gradually, we tackled more complex features like replacing the grids and file uploaders, and eventually overhauled the content and campaign creation flows. Along the way, we developed a comprehensive design system that ensures consistency and reusability for future updates.

New RXP Features

In addition to working on our long-term refresh project, we also dedicated resources to implementing new features. Some of these features, such as analytics, were requested by our Product Team, while others were identified and promoted by our design team to align with our business goals.

Analytics

During the ideation phase of our analytics feature, we made several breakthroughs, but the most significant one was shifting our primary focus from campaigns (referred to as engagements within the product) to content pages (called target pages in the product). We realized that while campaigns were central to the engineer's mental model, merchants primarily evaluate how their content is performing.

With this insight, we were able to design a comprehensive solution that enabled merchants of all sizes to answer critical questions and optimize their ROI. While our initial data collection was limited, we released a simplified first version and worked closely with the PMs to file improvement requests to gather the necessary data for a more complete solution.

Onboarding

Before merchants can start selling with Rezolve, they must configure several settings, including business details, payment collection, inventory, delivery, physical locations, and more. This process involves synchronizing with multiple systems, each with different response rates, which makes designing a self-serve onboarder quite challenging. While the initial version was functional, it required constant supervision and was not ready for independent use.

To address these issues, we revised the design to expose a more accurate mental model, setting the right expectations and helping users understand the process better. We simplified the process by eliminating any unnecessary configurations, progressively disclosing related settings, and using wording and visuals to acknowledge and manage user emotions.

After several iterations based on internal and external feedback, the final design performed exceptionally well in user testing, with all subjects completing the flow with ease.

Content creation

Small merchants faced difficulties coming up with content that looked good and performed well when faced with a black canvas. We collected solid evidence from production data and user testing to convince management that this was a problem worth solving.

To address this issue, we focused on low-hanging fruit, such as improving the empty state of the editor, enhancing editor usability, and providing generic templates that would suit the small merchant persona. While a more comprehensive solution that included easier and consistent brand implementation was considered, we decided to shelve it as part of our regular practice of prioritizing fast-paced development and measuring results rather than spending months polishing new features.

High-fidelity prototype testing confirmed that small merchants had a much-improved experience when starting from an empty canvas or from a template.

Shop Beautiful Redesign

To showcase the capabilities of the platform and sell the mobile SDK, the company had an app that needed a redesign. The original version had an outdated look and feel and a lot of technical debt, making it challenging to update with improvements and new features.

The redesign goals were to reimagine the explore surroundings experience, improve the image and audio scanning experience, and simplify the one-click purchasing flows for new and returning customers. The app needed to look modern, work smoothly, and be white-labelable to achieve business goals.

The redesign effort involved a significant focus on visual design and transitions to achieve a modern and smooth user experience. The final deliverables included a solid design system that enables customization and extensibility.

Instant Checkout MVP

Selling the mobile SDK can be a challenging task, which is why the company decided to introduce a web version of the Instant Buy feature to help ease the process. Despite having a tight deadline of just one week before the engineers began coding, the team followed a rigorous process and successfully aligned a cross-functional team. Sketches were used as a tool to facilitate thorough conversations and shape the architecture of the solution.

During this project, the visual design was guided by the chosen UI code components library, and the team aimed to deliver a product that was visually appealing, user-friendly, and easy to implement. Despite the high-pressure situation, the team was able to deliver a responsive webpage design that met all of these requirements.

Instant Checkout

During the development of the MVP version of the web Instant Checkout, we also worked on improving the design of the feature. Our goal was to create a more visually appealing UI and enhance the layout for larger screens.

To achieve this, we decided to change the approach from using dialogs for specific tasks such as adding a delivery address or a card, to using expanding and collapsing in-page sections. This allowed the user to remain focused on the subtask while still being able to stay within the visual context of the product page, creating a more seamless and simple user experience. Overall, we aimed to prioritize perceived simplicity in order to optimize the instant checkout experience.
