BE HEALTHY!

PROBLEM TO SOLVE

This is the project I did for the HCI online course from Coursera. Along with getting a completion certificate, this was a good way of practicing a lot of the theoretical skills learned in the class.

We had to make a choice between three broad themes and I decided to go with the CHANGE theme. So the project had to help users make a change in their lives. This fit me like a glove since I had a lot of interest during that time in improving my health and changing my lifestyle and I was curious to see if I can design a service that could help other people as well.

I had a pretty good idea of how the app would work for me especially because I knew my pain points during the whole change process but I could not base my design only on my experience and needs. So I went and observed and interviewed a few of my friends to see what their needs are and what would motivate them to make a real change.

The interviews and observations were focused around three activities that are important to health : eating, exercising and relaxing. Some of the participants were passionate about cooking, some about sports, some had no interest in any of them, so it was great to see different angles on the subject as well as very different pain points and motivating factors.

In a nutshell, the needfinding process revealed a lack of education in all of the three areas and also difficulties in getting and staying motivated to build healthy habits. In order to solve that, the design incorporates a lot of educational materials, gently displayed on most of the screen and makes heavy use of social interactions, social proof, smart notifications and large, beautiful images.

In order to keep myself anchored in the real context of the application usage during the design process, I created a couple of storyboards that sum up the interactions between the users and the service.

WIREFRAMES

USER TESTING

After the interactive prototypes were ready, I tested them with some of the users observed during the needfinding stage, as well as other users that haven't been exposed to the design discussions.

The feedback was mostly positive and some of the users seemed interested in using such a service on a daily basis.
