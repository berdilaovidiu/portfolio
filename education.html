I have spent the last ten years of my life developing an education around computer science. It was a childhood dream to become a computer programmer and I was fortunate to have the chance to study in a big city with lots of universities, like Cluj Napoca.

After college and a few years of professional programming, I fell in love with User Interface design. It happened when I met Bradford Paley at a seminar held on site by the company I was working for at that time. Since then, I read anything I could put my hands on related to UI design and tried to build as many skills as I could to make the transition to professional designing.

My formal education in design consists mostly in seminars, online classes and a lot of interesting books that cover a large variety of topics, from human cognition and psychology, to color theory and typography.

Oct 2013 - Jan 2014
Human-Computer Interaction online class
Intense class that teaches a human centered design metodology. Besides watching the lessons, I also had to do a lot of practice, going through each step of the process in order to deliver a functional dynamic prototype. I had to do user observations and interviews, storyboards, multiple versions of wireframes, heuristic evaluation, user testing, dynamic hi-fi prototypes and to review and grade the assignments of my peers.
This was a great learning experience that spread across three months of intensive study and practice. At the end, I was one of the less than 200 participants (from a total of more than 16000 attendants) who obtained a Statement of Accomplishment. The project is displayed in the projects section of this portfolio.
Bradford Paley
Sept 2011
Steps toward a Discipline of Cognitive Engineering seminar
It was during this class that I realized how much I enjoy designing solutions for human problems. Brad taught us the basics of a human-centered design methodology and more than that, he showed us some of his work with a passion that was contagious. After the seminar, the company hired him to redesign one of the products and I was part of the team that went to New York for a week to discuss the implementation of his ideas.
